Project Info

During a time when everyone is quarantining at home, we decided to spend our time at home creating visualizations to look at how the number of coronavirus cases per state have affected the Google search queries for that state.

Some patterns were expected, but others were very surprising. We present our visualizations to you so that you can easily analyze the data and create your own conclusions.

See Visualizations

State Map Overview

Our initial thought was to have a look at which states were most interested in the coronavirus-related search terms and if those periods of peaked interest were correlated to the number of confirmed coronavirus cases for that state.

The state map below shows all fifty states and it allows you to move through dates from January 1, 2020 until April 15, 2020. The shade of blue becomes darker for a state as the average coronavirus interest level for that state increases. You can hover over the states to see the total number of confirmed coronavirus cases for the chosen day.

This visualization is okay at providing a look at the coronavirus interest level, but unfortunately, it leaves it up to us to hover over a state to find any information about the confirmed cases for that state, so this is not the best visualization for this task.

Historical Trends vs Current Trends

Below you will find the Rising and Top queries from Google Trends for the month of March over the past five years for the entire USA. Use the arrows to switch between 17 different categories of search queries to see how each one has changed or stayed the same over time.

While we want to leave the analysis up to you, we found it very interesting how the patterns in certain categories for the Top queries remained very consistent over time up until a notable shift for in interest 2020. One specific example is the Top Queries over the past five years for the Hobbies & Leisure category.

Top Queries: These queries are search terms that have the largest number of hits, or searches. Typically, these queries remain consistent from year to year with little changes, but as we can see in the word clouds below, the year 2020 is disrupting the consistency in certain categories.

Rising Queries: These queries are search terms that have seen a significant increase interest, usually due to some current event or time of year. Rising queries may not have as many search totals as the Top queries, but they can be very useful in looking at what people have an interest in over a certain time.
